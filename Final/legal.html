Conclusions:

Evidence about the effect of marijuana legalization on overdose mortality and traffic safety is inconsistent and inconclusive. It remains unclear whether the presumed benefit of legalizing marijuana in reducing opioid-related harms outweighs the policy’s externalities, such as its on the productivity of it's users.
